How to Win at College

Writer and professor at Georgetown University, USA

Cal Newport

In his book, How to Win at College, Cal Newport has the following advice for students studying A Levels and degrees at university:


Sunday is the most important day of the week

Why?

"Sunday sets the tone for the week that follows.  

If you take control of your Sunday, you take control of your week."

What does 'taking control' of Sunday look like?

It's less like battling it into submission... ... and more like a self-care ritual

What do I need to do?

First set aside 30 minutes on Sundays for your pre-working week ritual.

Your 30 mins can be at any time of day that suits you: in the morning if that's when you're feeling energized; at 6pm before you relax for the evening...

But try and keep to the same time each week so that you're less likely to skip your ritual.

What should my Sunday night ritual include?

Let's go through this simple ABCD pattern one step at a time...

A is for activities

Write down what's coming up this week?

Is there a PE lesson or science practical that needs certain kit?

Do you need to prepare for a seminar or presentation?

Do you need to respond to an email or some teacher feedback?

Did you volunteer to help at a school event, club or sports fixture?

B is for bag

Are there homework tasks you need to hand in?

Worksheets, textbooks or other reading material which you need to take to lessons?

Write down what needs to go in your bag this week.

A book you borrowed from a teacher / friend / the library that you need to return?

Have you got the basics: pen, paper, calculator etc?

C is for crisis!

Is there anything you can deal with this week to avert a disaster?

a conversation you need to have with a teacher?

Be honest:  What's getting out of control at the moment?

a subject you're behind on?

a target or commitment you made in one subject to try to get back on track?

a request for help you need to make?

D is for deadlines

When are your homework tasks due this week (including the preparation or reading tasks for lessons, not just work to be handed in!)

Write down all of your deadlines for the coming week.

Are there tests coming up and you need to schedule revision time?

Is anything overdue?

Any other issues you need to sort out e.g. handing in consent forms, submitting EPQ or coursework proposal?

Let's get started.  Pretend it's Sunday evening...

Divide your page into four boxes like this

Next fill in each one in turn. 

Now its your turn!

Have a go at creating your Sunday night plan

Why is the ABCD approach helpful to my wellbeing as well as my organisation?

Using the ABCD structure as your Sunday night ritual will help calm your mind.  
 

You can kick back and relax on Sunday evenings, knowing you're ready for the week ahead.  You can sleep well, confident you're on top of things.  

"Get it right on Sunday and you'll start your week with momentum behind you."

Cal Newport
