The Catch-Up Week

SYSTEMS

Do you ever feel adrift??

Perhaps you've fallen behind where you want to be...

You might have let things slip, but now want to get back on track

What you might need is a catch-up week.

A catch-up week is a carefully planned and timetabled week where you work with much more organisation and focus.

 The aim is to plan like crazy, then throw yourself into a highly strategic seven days of concentration to recover all that lost ground.

You should start with a checklist of tasks you need complete - each of which gets crossed off when it’s done.

Catch up weeks need planning on paper first, with particular tasks assigned to particular days, even specific time-blocks.

Planning a week like this can feel daunting...

 so we’ve given you a simple place to start.

 First collect together all the work you want to catch up. 

Some will fill you with dread, others will be fairly straightforward. 

It can be really useful to arrange them according to how daunting each task is.

We have created the following example, based on a student studying 3 A Levels.

Most Daunting

  • Psychology: Review Lecture Slides - Attachment Theories

  • Sociology: Glossary of Terms - new terms related to Social Stratification

  • Maths: Complete Warm-up Exercises - algebra textbook's warm-up section.

  • Psychology: Watch a Documentary Clip - Milgram’s Obedience Experiment on YouTube.

  • Maths: Review Basic Formulas- Quadratic Equations and Probability on flashcards.

  • Psychology: Read and Annotate Journal Article - Cognitive Dissonance Theory

  • Sociology: Complete a Worksheet on Research Methods - qualitative vs. quantitative research

  • Maths: Solve Problem Sets - Calculus

  • Sociology: Draft an Essay Outline The "Impact of Media on Socialization."

  • Psychology: Complete Research Paper - The Influence of Childhood Experiences on Adult Relationships.

  • Sociology: Field Study - Design and implement a small-scale field study on Social Behavior in Public Spaces.

  • Maths: Prepare for a Major Exam - all topics Statistics and Probability

  • ​Psychology: Analyze Case Studies - Abnormal Psychology.

  • Maths: Master Advanced Topics - Differential Equations.

It's now time to plan your own catch up week.

You could follow our plan,
however this  becomes even more effective when you design your own plan

The idea of the week isn't to get everything on your list completed....

..instead the aim is to make a serious dent in your list, and get you back on track.

We’ve suggested a ‘one big task per day’ structure, shown in our example week.

Final Thoughts

  • If you can take action on these tasks as the week goes by, you’ll be significantly closer to living that stress-free, pressure-free life we all dream of!

  • So every time you feel demotivated or overwhelmed, forget the big picture.

  • Instead, make a plan for a super-organised catch-up week.

  • Return to this activity or design one of your own, using the advice below to help you…

    • Some students plan a week like this at the start of every half term to get them up and running

    • Some students have alternated weeks like this, one on, one off.

    • Towards exam season or coursework deadlines, we’ve seen students plan two or three weeks of organised and time-blocked activities to keep them on track.

    • The best plans we’ve seen include rewards, with students scheduling something good to keep them going.
