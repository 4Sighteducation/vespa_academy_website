Night School

SYSTEMS

What is 'night school'?

There used to be night schools across the country - regular schools and colleges that taught school-age students in the day, but which opened their doors to teach adults at night.  

Night schools were very different to normal school days:

  • they only lasted an hour or two

  • no uniform

  • no assembly or tutor time

  • no one using 'Sir' or 'Miss'

But there were other more subtle differences...

No one was making students turn up.  Tutors wouldn't chase them for work or even keep a check on attendance. But...

Students wanted to be there and wanted to achieve so...

  • they were enthusiastic

  • they asked lots of questions

  • they were focused and didn't allow themselves to be distracted by phones or other people

  • they reviewed their notes in their own time to consolidate learning

  • they did the preparatory reading and always came prepared

You won't be building it out of lego (complete with waterslide, pets room, waterslide etc!)

..Instead you're designing the routines of your school:

  • What days does it run?

  • When do classes start and finish?

  • Where does it take place?

  • What nights does it run?

You're going to design your own night school

You're also designing the school ethos (its values, culture and behaviour)

  • What is and is not good learning behaviour?

  • How will you ensure students are engaged and focused?

  • How will you make hard or dull topics interesting?

  • What sorts of learning activities will you use to keep students engaged?

Activities and Resources

What kinds of activities would be happening in your night school to make it active and interesting?

Would you use some of the following, or are there others you'd like to try?

  • videos to watch

  • new ways of taking notes

  • colourful summaries and knowledge organisers

  • displays with helpful reminders about key content

  • mini whiteboards

  • self-quizzing and testing

  • discussions, explanations or quizzing with peers

Write a list of all of the different types of learning activities you currently use.

 

  • Which are the most effective?

  • Which do you like the most?

  • Which would you want to use in your night school - and which would you ditch?

Rituals and Rewards

What routines or traditions might you want to have in your school?

How do you want to start each lesson?

  • with a quiz

  • a tough question

  • with a discussion point

  • with music

  • with a reminder of the last lesson?

How will you end each session?

  • with another quiz

  • with a teaser or preview of the next topic

  • with a summary / recap of the session's learning

What new routines do you want?

  • low level music in the background

  • a high energy dance interval between lessons

  • regular biscuit breaks

Rituals and Rewards

Don't forget the rewards!  How will you recognise progress?

  • scorecards

  • charts to show improved marks

  • calendars that show an unbroken study streak

  • the snack of your choice

  • the game, film or TV show you've been looking forward to

Timetable

When would your night school run?

  • 90 mins on Tue and Thur?

  • Once a week but for 2 hours?

  • Straight after school or college between 4pm and 5.30pm?

  • Or does it start later after you've had a chance to recharge, running 7-8.30pm?

What are the benefits or drawbacks of each?

Now what times have you decided on?

Timetable

How are you going to break down the time?

  • One full hour followed by some admin / filing notes / getting organised for the next session?

  • Shorter 30 minute chunks with 5 min refresher breaks in between?

Depending on the task, shorter chunks of time can be more effective than one long session.

See the Pomodoro technique

Campus

Now it's time to design your learning environment.

What does your classroom look like?  Are you in a branch of a coffee shop?  Or a much quieter library?  Is there a suitable space at your school/college which can be used after school hours?  Is it your bedroom?

Wherever it is, can you modify the environment a little to indicate that night school is in session?  e.g.

  • remove unnecessary items from your desk

  • rearrange the furniture

  • switch off your phone

  • put a sign on the door!

Night school term has started!

Now that you've got some ideas, try running your night school for two weeks.  You'll be the only student and you'll be the teacher too.  

Plan some simple sessions reviewing notes or recapping day school topics.  It won't be long before you're getting ahead on your work.  

You'll understand things more in class and you should feel more in control of your work and less stressed.  

Why not do a school exchange?

Run your night school just for yourself for a couple of weeks.  

Then, if it's working, invite a friend.  They can try the routines and techniques that are working at your school...

...and you can visit their night school and do the same!
