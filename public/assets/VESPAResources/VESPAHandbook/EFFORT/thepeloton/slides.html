What is the peloton?

By riding close together cyclists conserve energy. With less air resistance, it feels easier.  

How is this like a classroom?

Why is it useful?

In most classrooms, there are a few cyclists (students) working very hard and they're at the front of the pack.   

There may be a few at the back, doing little work and at risk of falling further and further behind.  

Most students in the pack work hard enough to get by but are not putting in maximum effort - after all, GCSEs or A Level are a long race, not a sprint.  

At some point, individual riders decide to go for it.  They put in a burst of effort, break away from the pack and give themselves the chance of winning the race.

How is this like approaching exams? 

As exams approach, some students decide to put in more effort.  The result is that they go faster in the race - make more progress - and end up crossing the finish line with a much better result.

Are you really in the peloton?

Being honest with yourself

It's easy to look at our group of friends and convince ourselves that, because we're doing a similar amount of work, that we must be working an average amount.  

However......

 

try to think about the class as a whole ...

Research has found that students tend to surround themselves with people doing similar amounts of work to them. 

Your friends will find it reassuring to think that others are doing the same as them - even if actually the rest of the class is doing more! 

It becomes like a self-fulfilling prophecy!!
