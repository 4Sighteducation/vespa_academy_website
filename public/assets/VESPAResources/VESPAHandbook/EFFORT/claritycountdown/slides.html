Comprehensive 

Every teacher is convinced their course is the most important!!

Students have to deal with hundreds if not thousands of pieces of information per week.

New content appears every day - new ideas, new topics, new vocabulary… it can be exhausting just keeping up.

New ideas arrive and we haven’t even figured out the old ones....

But it can get way worse if we fall behind.

...new topics are started but we don’t fully understand the bits we’re supposed to have finished.

Over time, these tricky, half-understood topics can build up and build up…

Pretty soon there’s huge amounts of stuff that we haven’t got our heads around.

It can feel hugely overwhelming and stressful.

If this describes you, the following activity could be really helpful.

Begin by attending a class and staying alert. Listen carefully to the content, engage with the topic and…

…as soon as something crops up that you don’t understand, imagine a countdown clock starts ticking.

FINAL THOUGHTS

By trying a clarity countdown, you might put yourself under a little bit more pressure on the day, but you ensure that problems don’t build up.​

Why not try...

  • Use a clarity countdown for a particular subject

    • ​Commit to doing this in a specific class, or tricky run of lessons

    • Simply reserve a few minutes at the end of each day, and keep it up until you are through the challenging section.

  • Using a clarity countdown for all subjects, but for a short period of time

    • This works particularly well in the run-up to test, mock exams or even the real thing.

    • It can be an intense week or fortnight, but you end it knowing you’re absolutely on top of everything.
