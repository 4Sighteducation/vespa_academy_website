Hate being interrupted?

You might like some interruptions - for example your friend stopping by to say hello, a notification on your phone of a new message, or  someone sharing a funny video with you.

And you might dislike others - your younger sibling making noise, your parents telling you to do a chore or  the dog being sick on your carpet.

Gloria Mark, Professor of Infomatics at the University of California

...Researches the 'disruption cost' i.e. the amount of time or energy we lose because of interruptions to our work.

It's not just the time taken up by the interruption.  It takes us up to 25 minutes to fully return our attention to a task after an interruption.  

Mark conducted an experiment with university students.  They had to work through an email inbox.  One group had no interruptions...

 

What harm do interruptions do?  Isn't it nice to say hello or have a quick chat before returning to our work?

... the other group were frequently interrupted by people.
Mark measured how long it took the students to complete the task. 

All students completed the task in roughly the same amount of time - 20 minutes.  

So no problem, right?  The task still got completed.  

The results?

Students who had been interrupted experienced significantly higher levels of:

Not quite.  Mark also measured how students felt during the task.  This is what she found...

  • stress

  • frustration

  • time pressure

  • workload

  • tiredness

How do I avoid stressful distractions?

By designing your study sessions differently, you can avoid the frustration and stress that comes with interruptions.

Try not to:

  • keep switching tasks or topics

  • work in places where you'll get sidetracked or disturbed

Instead, you're aiming to for DEEP WORK i.e. work with concentrated effort on the same thing for a period of time.

In his book, 'Deep Work', the academic Cal Newport argues that tricky, demanding tasks require us to work deeply.

Deep work does not require a snorkel, but it does require:

  • extended periods of concentration

  • prolonged effort

  • hard thinking

What is 'deep work'?

Make your list of deep work tasks now.  Choose tasks that are going to require HIGH levels of effort - tasks you'd love to get done without distraction or stress.
