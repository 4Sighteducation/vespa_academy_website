CLASS POLL

If you had to take an important exam, and only had 1 hr to prepare, which of the following revision methods would you use:

  1. Read through the information once

  2. Read through the information once, cover it and write out as much as possible from memory

  3. Read through the information 4 times

  4. Read through the information once, then create a mind-map of the information

A fascinating study from 2011 placed students in different groups to revise for a test using these 4 methods

Here is one of our favourite podcasters, Ali Abdaal, discussing the study...

Here is what actually happened...

Retrieval Practice - "Read through the information once, cover it and write out as much as possible from memory" was the best method for all types of questions

Closed-book Notetaking:

  1. Read the section of textbook/information whilst highlighting the key information. Really connect and concentrate as you read and highlight.

  2. Now close the book/put the notes away.

  3. Now write notes on the section you’ve just covered without looking at the information!  This will feel hard. You might get frustrated. You won’t be able to remember everything. No problem; leave lots of space to add forgotten information. Scatter the notes around the page with subheadings and leave lots of white paper.

  4. When you’re done, open the book or turn over the notes. Re-read and note-take as you go but…

  5. …add the stuff you missed or forgot in another colour, filling the white space you left first time around.

An active recall study technique in five steps

First, you’ll need to choose something you want to learn.

You’ll need a section of textbook – not too long –

..or a study guide, or some notes you’ve already made.

Once you’ve chosen what you’re aiming to learn, here’s what to do:

  1. Read the section of textbook/information whilst highlighting the key information.

  2. Now close the book/put the notes away.

  3. Now write notes on the section you’ve just covered without looking at the information!  

  4. When you’re done, open the book or turn over the notes.

  5. …add the stuff you missed or forgot in another colour, filling the white space you left first time around.

We suggest you try it now to see how well it works..

Try reading some recent class notes or a text book for 5 minutes

Next put the notes away...

..Take a blank sheet of paper and make notes, from memory, for a further 5 mins, seeing how much you can remember

FINAL THOUGHTS

  • This approach will be more effective than reading the material four times.

  • You might even finish more quickly than you would have doing four re-reads.

  • Of course, it will feel harder than just re-reading.

  • It’s not as comfortable, and you might feel exhausted by the end.

  • But you’ll perform better in tests and exams if you make this part of your weekly study!
