ATTITUDE

In her book Rapt, she writes about attention in the modern world

She examines the psychology of attention, particularly why we pay attention to the things we do.

Gallagher, Winifred, Rapt, Penguin 2010

Winifred Gallagher 

is a science writer, magazine editor and journalist.

Link to the full video: https://muse.ai/v/L5sgt8N

The following video summarises some of Gallagher's recommendations...

"We’re in control of where we spend our attention, not them. We can choose what to pay attention to."

In her book, Gallagher states: 

“…the quality of your life depends not on fame or fortune, beauty or brains, fate or coincidence, but on what you choose to pay attention to.”

She goes on to argue that,

To what extent do you agree?

Tempting as it is to focus on your family or home or friends, if you’re lucky, they’re going to be there every day.

 Instead, you’re looking for something that specifically occurred on the day you’re thinking about. 

In this activity, we’re going to suggest you spend one working week deliberately paying attention to positive things. 

Your aim is to collect three positive things each day.

A Week of Positive Things..

A couple of rules:

  • You don’t need to be winning the lottery; your observation can be very modest, just a small change, and

  • Your observation has to be specific to that day. 

Some areas of your life to think about:

Was there just one moment in a lesson today that was positive?

1. Your lessons:

  •  A bad joke, an interesting video, an activity that was OK?

  • A word from a teacher that was positive?

  • A smiley face scribbled in an exercise book?

  • A topic you understood and felt good about? 

  • A friend saying something funny or nice

  • Someone holding a door open for you?

  • A smile from a teacher or an arm around the shoulder from a friend?

  • A member of the canteen staff saying hello?

Was there a connection today that was positive?

2. Your interactions:

  • was there a break in the rain and the sun came out?

  • A warm classroom after a cold break outside?

  • A decent game of netball in the gym or football on the astroturf?

  • A comfy chair in a common room or study space?

Was there a something positive in your surroundings today?

3. Your surroundings:

  • was there a moment on the bus on the way in that brightened your day?

  • A chat while you walked between lessons?

  • A discussion in the lunch queue or a walk with a friend? 

  • Did you see  a cute dog and stroke it!?

Was there a something positive in any of your journeys today?

4. Your journeys:

  • I understood the maths problem we were working on, and it felt good to get it right without help.

  • My friend complimented my new backpack, which made me smile.

  • The sun came out during lunch break, so we could sit outside and enjoy the warm weather.

Mon

  • The history teacher showed a funny video clip related to our topic, and it made the whole class laugh.

  • A classmate noticed I was having trouble with my locker and offered to help me open it. 

  • I had a nice chat with a friend on the bus ride home about our favorite movies.

Tue

  • I felt really good when the English teacher praised my essay in front of the class.

  • My friend saved me a seat in the cafeteria, so I didn’t have to look for a seat.

  • It was raining all morning, but it stopped just in time for us to have PE outside.

Wed

  • I got a smiley face sticker on my science homework, which made me feel proud.

  • The librarian remembered my name and recommended a book she thought I’d like.

  • I walked between classes with a friend, and we had a fun conversation about our weekend plans.

Thur

  • We did a group activity in geography that was actually really interesting, and my group worked well together.

  • A teacher gave me a thumbs up when I answered a question correctly in class.

  • The classroom was warm and cozy after coming in from the cold outside, which made it easier to concentrate.

Fri

Reading over the diary at the end of the week will help you feel a lot more positive about your studies...

Here is an example from one of the students we have worked with recently...

Have a go at creating a positive mood diary

Keep a record of 3 positive things occuring each day...

...try also adding an emoji summary for the whole day.

Have a go now to see if you can write down 3 positive things that have happened today (or yesterday)...
