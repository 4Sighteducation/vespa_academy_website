NAF vs NAch

ATTITUDE

NAF vs NAch

1. NAF

2. NAch

  • They may avoid Challenges for fear of failing

  • They might avoid responsibility

  • They're easily dissuaded from taking part or finishing work

  • They don't like assessments

  • They want success but their fear of failure outweighs their desire to succeed

  • They avoid situations where they might fail, ie. not handing in work, telling others how little work they've done

  • They thrive on Challenge

  • They're often determined workers who take risks, failure isn't a problem.

  • They enjoy the challenge of assessment

  • They can take low grades in their stride, and move on

  • Even though they may feel some fear, they will still put themselves in situations where they might fail

NAF

NAch

This student is struggling with understanding the work but won’t ask questions in class to clarify their learning. When you ask why, they say they ‘feel embarrassed’.

You gave this student a grade D for piece of work two lessons ago. They arrive this lesson with the piece of work re-done, and ask if you’d mind marking it again.

When you organise some group work, this student asks to work with a group of students who are doing much better than they are.

ACTIVITY:

The following two lists show actual behaviours associated with

ACTIVITY 2 -

  • Now read through the NACH list of behaviours

  • Note down those that you are not currently doing

  • Finally commit to trying 2 or 3 new behaviours

FINAL THOUGHTS

  • Being more aware of specific behaviours you wish to improve can have a huge impact on your ultimate success.

  • Choose behaviours to address which look possible – the kind of thing you could integrate into your working week

  • Make a commitment to give them a try.

  • They might not have an immediate impact, but they could be the start of improving grades!
