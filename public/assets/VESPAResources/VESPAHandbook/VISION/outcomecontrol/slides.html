Watch this for an example of someone achieving  goals!

But HOW did David Beckham become one of the world's wealthiest, most famous, most successful footballers?  

That is not the goal he set himself.  There were too many things beyond his control:

  • which club would offer him a contract,

  • could he stay injury-free,

  • how good were the other players coming through the youth system.  

Instead, he focused on the things within his control:

  • the amount of time he put into practice

  • his fitness levels

  • improving the accuracy of his shots 

Sometimes we get the advice to make our goals as specific as possible.   

..But that might not work if the outcome isn't within our control

Here are a couple of examples....

2 - I want to be the principal clarinettist in the New York Philharmonic Orchestra?

There's only one such role in the whole world

It's VERY specific but too far beyond your control.

1 - I want to be a lifestyle influencer with 2 million subscribers and an annual turnover of £100k?

This is specific, but it doesn't make it more likely to happen!

1. circle of control - we can take meaningful action to make a difference to outcomes

2. circle of influence - things we can influence through our efforts but cannot control the outcome

3. circle of concern - external factors we care about but cannot influence

Thinking about what you can control can help your wellbeing too

Focusing our goals on the inner circle of control is most beneficial for both achieving them and our wellbeing, with some of our energy being spent on the circle of influence.
