What is Ikigai?

konnichiwa

shashimi

ikigai

hello

raw fish delicacy

purpose in life

empty hands

How to pronounce it?  

iki = life

gai = worth

ikigai = life's worth

icky-guy

Imagine it's Monday morning and you're thinking about the week ahead...

It's probably because you haven't found your ikigai yet.

Ever felt like this?

Alternatively, ever had a day where you've leapt out of bed because you were keen to get started?

What were you about to do?

Go on a trip?

Meet someone special?

Watch or attend an event?

You're trying to find something elusive and valuable...

...not treasure

...not a lost city

...not a mythical beast

...but purpose in life, 

a reason for being, something to make you happier every day

A quest to find your ikigai

But how do you find your sense of purpose?

Your ikigai map...

These are internal factors.  They're about YOU.

These are external factors.  They're about the world around you.

First, fold a piece of paper in half.  

How to make the map work for you

  • When are you happiest?

  • What do you get excited about?

  • What are you passionate about?

  • What could you talk for hours about?

  • What always makes you feel better?

  • What do you enjoy spending time doing?

In the left hand column, list topics, hobbies, subjects, activities etc that you really enjoy.  

Step 1 - List what you love

In the right hand column, write what you're good at.  Think about school work but also 'soft skills' like dealing with people, being resourceful etc.

You don't need to be an expert, the best or world class!  

Good-ish or good enough is fine.

Step 2 - List what you're good at

  • Where have you made a difference to a group or team?

  • What would your friends say are your strengths?

Is there anything you love and you're good at?  

Step 3 - Are there any connections between your two lists?

Try to draw links between them and explain to a friend how they're connected.

Congratulations - you might have found your PASSION!

They might not be identical but are there connections between them?

Now it's time to think about the external factors that will help you find your ikigai....

 Step 4 - What can you be paid for?

Highlight on your list anything which might lead to paid work.

  • Eating cake

  • Playing computer games

  • Playing with the dog

  • Restaurant critic; pastry chef

  • Games designer; Technology journalist

  • Dog trainer; animal welfare officer

They may not realistically provide a paid job.  But what related jobs might be paid?

This might be your list of things you love and are good at....

  • freedom

  • flexibility

  • creativity

  • autonomy

  • risk

  • challenge

With your highlighted list, consider what else these jobs might provide:

Are some of these more important to you than money?

Is there anything on your list that the world needs more of.....

healthcare workers

climate change experts

engineers

researchers

cyber security specialists

entrepreneurs

Step 6 - Take a rest. 

You won't suddenly find the perfect job idea.  But you might find clues to set you on the right path.

Your ikigai might be to:
