Lifestyle Envy vs Job Envy

VISION

What would make you jealous?

Imagine a friend telling you about a new opportunity they're about to take up.  They're excited, and they're describing to you what they're about to begin doing.  The more you listen, the more you become insanely jealous.  

It doesn't have to be a specific job. Don't worry about how realistic it is. Just note down the sorts of things that would get your envy flowing...

Think about...

  • working hours

  • working conditions

  • a typical working day

  • the place of work

  • kinds of tasks and responsibilities

  • the rewards on offer

  • further opportunities that might follow

  • status

The secret to real job satisfaction?

To be truly content, it has to be the work itself that is meaningful and important to you.  

So if you've got nothing in the job envy column, have another go at the activity.

Try to focus on what kind of work would make you envious.  Keep it broad and general if it helps

FINAL THOUGHTS

  • If you find you have the lifestyle column full and the job column empty – don’t worry, this happens to us all sometimes

  • We can all get confused between lifestyle and job.

  • We’ve seen students pursuea job or career because of the lifestyle they imagine it will give them, not because of the work itself...

  •  …the student who applies to be a lawyer because they’ve seen legal dramas on TV and the after-work parties look glamourous and attractive…

  • …or the student who wants to become an archaeologist because they imagine the travelling will be exciting, but haven’t thought about the hours of back-breaking digging in the hot sun…

  • …or the student applying to be a teacher because “the holidays are great.”
