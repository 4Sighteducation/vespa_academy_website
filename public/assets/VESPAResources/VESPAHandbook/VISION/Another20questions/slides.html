Another 20 QUESTIONS

VISION

Comprehensive 

Vision is a picture of the future that produces passion.”

- Bill Hybels

The only thing worse than being blind, is having  sight but no vision.”

Helen Keller, political activist, author

...what is your life goal?

...so what do you want to do with your life?

It turns out that asking “what is your goal?” isn’t a very good way of unlocking your vision.

The answer lies in asking yourself lots of different questions…

The following 20 questions have been grouped in fives. Aim to answer 2-3 questions per 5 (10 in total)

 

Which in turn, help you answer the main one!

 

Try and answer the questions without feeling embarrassed or stupid, say what you really feel and think.

 

The question is abstract and slippery, and answering it is often embarrassing and frightening...

Another Twenty Questions...

  • Think of a friend who isn’t doing a subject you are. What enjoyable class would you have them sit in to show them what they’re missing?

  • List five topics you’ve covered recently – from most to least interesting

  • If you wanted to persuade a year 11 pupil about a subject you take, what would you say are its strengths?

  • What topic (any topic in the world! From a class, or from your own life) do you know so well you could teach a 30-minute lesson on it with less than five minutes’ preparation?

  • If you could specify the homework given to you, what kind of tasks might you ask for?

COLLEGE LIFE

Another Twenty Questions...

  • If you had to take up a new hobby what would it be?

  • Imagine you had to start and run an after-college club. What would it be about?

  • What club/group would you feel most at home in?

  • List three clubs you used to attend after primary/secondary school. Do you still have interests in this area?

  • What is an activity that other people seem obsessed with but that you just can’t see the point of? And is there something that interests you that others might not understand?

OUT OF COLLEGE INTERESTS

Another Twenty Questions...

  • If you were a billionaire, what would you spend your money doing once you’d got yourself everything you’ve ever wanted?

  • If you had no family, who would you leave all your money to – and why?

  • If you had a million pounds, what could you do with it to positively impact the most people?

  • What kind of work are you absolutely determined not to do for a living?

  • If all jobs had the same pay and same working hours, what job would you pick?

HYPOTHETICALS (A)

Another Twenty Questions...

  • What problem(s) do you see in the world around you that you might like to help solve?

  • Imagine you’re at the end of your working life. What would you regret not trying to achieve?

  • What ingredients make a good life?

  • What do you regret not starting when you were younger?

  • if an extra day of the week was created, and it had to be for ‘improving your future’, what activities might you plan to do?

HYPOTHETICALS (B)

ROUND 1

5 mins

Decide who is going to answer the questions first.

  • This activity works well in pairs, so buddy up with a friend.

  • Pick a selection of questions to ask your partner while they do the same. (Try to aim for at least 10)

  • Take it in turns as the “life coach”, and ask your questions to your partner, listening carefully to the answers.

  • (You MUST remain silent when listening to the response!)

  • Switch roles, perhaps try some different questions?

  • Give your partner your written responses to their questions.

  • Often seeing things written down can really help you understand your vision!

BIG IDEA

  • If you work 40 hours a week from age 20-65 you will work just over 90,000 hours in your lifetime.

  • Time is a non-renewable resource; once spent, it can never be earnt back.

  •  It’s definitely worth taking some time to consider your Vision for the future!
