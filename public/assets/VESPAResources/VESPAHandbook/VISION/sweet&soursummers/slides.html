Chris Sacca's story..

Entrepeneur, Lawyer and Investor, 

Chris Sacca

Chris Sacca is a prominent figure known for his roles as a lawyer, investor, and former Google employee. Sacca credits his success to the vales instilled in him when he was younger

His parents made him work during every summer holiday!

Entrepeneur, Lawyer and Investor, 

Chris Sacca

Sacca described his summer jobs as "sweet and sour.".....

During the first few weeks of his summer break, he would engage in a job he wanted to do. Although the work was challenging, his interest made it enjoyable.

These were the "sweet" weeks characterised by engaging tasks and positive interactions with colleagues.

Conversely, the following weeks involved jobs he didn't desire, leading to the "sour" experience of repetitive and often uninteresting work. Sacca credits these contrasting experiences with providing him significant advantages over peers lacking similar life experiences.

Text

Sweet Jobs

Sour Jobs

Your list of sweet jobs....

  • skills required
  • teamwork / individual
  • particular field e.g. IT, science, creative arts?

Do they have anything in common?

Or if you prefer, you can send a SHORT email.  The boss's time is precious!

You have 5 lines to sell yourself.  Go!

Get that sweet job!  

Imagine you're in a lift with the boss of the place you want to work. You have 30 secs to convince them to give you work experience.  

...The Elevator Pitch

You should include..

  • an introduction to yourself and what your interests are

  • what you're hoping to get out of your summer work

  • specific projects or parts of the work you'd love to be involved in.

“Find a job you love, and you will never have to work a day in your life.”  Mark Twain

FINAL THOUGHTS

  • Are there any themes emerging from your imaginary job choices?

  • Think about whether your sweet jobs have certain similarities, or your sour jobs do.

  • These might be clues to pursue when you get to
    thinking about how you want to earn your money in future.

  • Finally – push yourself to imagine trying to get one of your ‘sweet’ jobs. Is it possible?

  • What employers near where you live might take you on?

  • Is there someone you could speak to? You never know, there might be a future career waiting for you…
